I. Introduction

Multiplier are a crucial component of digital signal processing (DSP) systems. Some of the design targets are high speed, accuracy, low power consumption, the regularity of layout and less area. Commonly, the execution speed of multipliers depends on two factors, semiconductor technology and multiplier architecture.

A 180 nm design parameter is discussed as it is widely used nowadays. The main motivation of this project is to design a digital circuit integrated device and implementing it on the simulation tools, i.e., Virtuoso of Candence. To make an implementation of the EDA tools is quite useful for further digital and analog design.

The 4x4-bits multiplier is a circuit with two 4-bit unsigned numbers as inputs, and the 8-bit product as output, which will perform the multiplication of the two input. There are many DSP applications, where critical delay path and the performance of the processor lies in the multiplier. 4x4 array multiplier is an advanced one which is described in this article.

D. 4*4-bit Array Multiplier

A 4*4-bit array multiplier is a circuit with two 4-bit unsigned numbers as inputs, and 8-bit product of these two numbers as the output. The multiplier will perform the multiplication of two 4-bit input.

 

Fig. 7. Schematic view of 4*4-bit array multiplier

As Fig.7 shown, the 4*4-bit array multiplier consists of 16 logic ANDs, 4 half adders and 8 full adders. There are 8 inputs named and respectively, and also 8 outputs named and . Among these, , and is LSB of its own sign number.

The number of critical (longest) delay paths in the 4x4-bit array multiplier is 4. Fig. 8 shows one of the 4 paths. The critical delay path should be from the input or or or to the output . or The number of logic gates passed sum up to 1 logic gate AND, 1 half adder and 7 full adders when operating the 4x4-bit array multiplier. By analyzing the logic function of each gate, we can find that the input vectors that excite these propagation delay paths are that equals 1111 and equals 1111.

Fig. 8. One of the critical (longest) delay paths

So as to get the propagation delay, we measure the propagation delay of the 4*4-bit array multiplier with schematic level simulation in Cadence. We set equals 1111 and changes from 0000 to 1111 to get the longest rising delay. Then we reverse the changes 1111 to 0000 and remain the others unchanged so that we get the longest falling delay. The 8 outputs all have 50 fF extrinsic load capacitor respectively. The measuring results are shown in Fig. 9 below. The rising delay is 1.311 ns and falling delay is 0.902 ns. Therefore, the critical propagation delay equals the sum of rising delay and falling delay divided by 2 is 1.106 ns.

E. 4*4-bit Array Multiplier Layout Area and Delay

The layout of 4*4-bit array multiplier is shown below in Fig.10. It consists of 8 INV gates, 8 NAND gates,

4 half adders and 8 full adders, which costs the area of 2618.4897(L=22.58, W=115.965)

Fig. 10. layout of 4*4-bit array multiplier

To measure the post-layout propagation delay, we adopt the same method and parameters in schematic measurement. In cadence, each product output drives 50 fF extrinsic load capacitor and inputs equals 1111 and changes from 0000 to 1111 and 1111 to 0000. Fig 11 shows the measure results. The rising delay is 1.669 ns and falling delay is 1.156ns. Therefore, the post-layout propagation delay is 1.41 ns.

Fig. 11. Rising delay and falling delay of the post-layout

Compared the post-layout simulation result from Fig.11 to the schematic simulation in Fig.9, it can be found that the overall delays of schematic level are smaller than ones of post-layout level. The critical propagation delay in schematic is 0.304ns smaller the critical propagation delay in post-layout, which is 1.106ns versus 1.41ns.Among these, the rising delay difference is 0.388ns smaller according to the schematic of 1.699ns and post-layout of 1.311ns. The falling delay difference is 0.254ns smaller by the schematic of 0.902ns and the post-layout of 1.156ns.

The difference exists due to the limitation of the schematic level circuit simulation tool. The post-layout simulation is based on the extraction, which involves parasitic capacitance and resistance, but schematic level simulation only involves the initial external load capacitance. In practice, the load capacitance contains gate capacitance, source/drain capacitance, parasitic capacitance from polysilicon and metals. After taking these extra capacitors and parasitic resistors into account, the post-layout level delay is larger than the schematic level delay is explained.

Therefore, the accurate resistance and capacitance extraction for correct delay estimation is essential for simulating the operation of device in real. Wiring forms a complex geometry that introduces capacitive, resistive and inductive parasite, which has impact on delay, energy consumption, power distribution and introduce noise sources that affects reliability. And post-layout extraction aims to calculate the parasite capacitance and resistance caused during wiring and add them together. The accurate resistance and capacitance extraction ensures that it can simulate the circumstance closest to the reality and estimate the correct delay.

Based on this project, we have more comprehension in SME306 course and basically master the skills of Cadence Virtuoso. Although the multiplier is a fundamental element with simple structure in digital circuits design, its internal structure and wiring method is not a simple task and worth considering meticulously. The area and the delay are relative. One will increase as the other one decreases so the trade-off should be considered prudently to optimize our design. And such plenty of time it cost us to decide the size of standard cell layout. Besides the trade-off, the process of wiring layout is an another dilemma. But after we are familiar with the layout design, the bugs reported apparently decrease and pass the DRC and LVS. The simulation and analysis part need us to be careful with the testbench file. Once it changes a bit, the whole result goes wrong.

After our first completion of our project, we realize that our design still has many drawbacks compared with others. We can get a better trade-off between layout and propagation delay by minimizing the size of transistors further. So we redo the project from start with basic cell layout design and finally get a satisfying result.

All in all, we have learnt the digital COMS design flow, the steps of schematic design, layout design, simulation, and performance analysis. Study has no limit. We should spend more time in practicing our design skills.
